## Day 1 - This project.

This is a side project I've decided to recently start for when I'm a little bored. I don't like how people are getting less and less creative, so I want to create a portfolio that showcases how people can create what *they* want to make.

People are too caught up with getting into university, getting a job, they're not appreciating building stuff or doing stuff that *they* want to do. This website will be a cool experiment where it's just me adding what *I* want to add to my website, hopefully creating a cool illusion.

## Day 2 - Thought

Thought is kind of underestimated in the modern world. People think too quickly and apprehensively instead of just thinking on something before doing. I really like thinking hard and researching on my projects before making them and this project is no different.

Now there's a couple things I absolutely must have on my website...

The first thing is large images of my projects that are insanely easy to access. It should be the main focus of my website. You load in and you just immediately see all the cool things I've built. I really like this style of website for showcasing the projects, but I want to make it even easier to access, so probably do like a scroll effect instead https://www.kevinvalete.com/

The second thing that is a must have is having a blog/place for me to journal my thoughts. I really like sites like https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog where people can just directly see what I'm up to, thought I'm not a big fan of each section taking up lots of space.

The third thing I need is lots of information actually about me. I don't want this to feel like a cold place and that I'm a heartless person so I need to be able to put decent size chunks of text actually about me. I really love https://kognise.dev/ , the combination of large chunks of text and a clean blog is very nice. The navigation is also very simple, and it takes you through like an adventure.
